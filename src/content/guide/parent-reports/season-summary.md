## What is a season summary?

A season summary is a special report that pulls everything from the year together into one document. Where individual game reports focus on a single performance, the season summary zooms out and gives you the full picture — how your athlete developed from the first game to the last.

Think of it as the year-end version of the game report. It is written to give you a sense of the journey your child went through over the course of the season, not just what happened on any one Friday night.

## What it includes

The season summary typically covers:

- **Season-long stats** — totals and averages across every game your athlete played
- **Overall narrative** — a written overview from the coach reflecting on your athlete's development, effort, and growth areas
- **Growth trajectory** — a look at how key stats trended over the course of the season, so you can see improvement in context

## How it differs from game reports

Individual game reports are published after each game and reflect that specific performance. The season summary is different — it is cumulative. It shows patterns that only become visible when you look at the whole season together. An athlete who struggled early but finished strong will have that arc reflected in the summary.

## When it appears

The season summary appears on your athlete's profile page after the coach marks the season as complete. It is labeled "Season" to distinguish it from individual game report cards, which are labeled by opponent or game date.

If you have not yet seen a season summary and the season feels like it has ended, it may simply mean your coach has not yet marked it complete. Coaches sometimes finalize reports a few weeks after the last game.

## Where to find it

Open your athlete's profile (tap the Player Profile tab in the bottom navigation), then scroll through the reports section. The season summary will appear at the top when it is available, since it represents the full season's work.
