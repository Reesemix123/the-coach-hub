## Where to find reports

Tap the **Player Profile** tab at the bottom of the screen, then scroll down to the **Game Reports** section. Each card in this section represents a report from a specific game.

The card shows the opponent's name, the game date, and a brief summary line. Tap the card to expand it and read the full report.

## What the report includes

Each game report contains a narrative written specifically about your child's performance in that game. It highlights what they did well — the plays they made, the effort they showed, the moments where they stood out — and offers one area for growth to help them continue developing.

The tone is warm and encouraging. These reports are written with family in mind, so the language is straightforward and positive, not filled with technical jargon. You should be able to read it with your child and feel good about sharing it.

The coaching staff sees a more detailed, technical version of the same analysis. What you receive is a family-friendly version designed to keep you informed and connected to your child's progress without getting into the deeper film room details.

## What "Published" means

You'll notice a **Published** badge on reports that are ready for you to read. This means your coach has reviewed the report and intentionally shared it with your family. Reports don't appear automatically — they go through a review step so coaches can make sure the content is accurate and complete before it reaches you.

## Why a game might not have a report yet

If a game doesn't have a report card in your list, there are a couple of common reasons:

- Your coach may still be working through film analysis for that game. Film review takes time, especially after a long travel game or a week with multiple events.
- Your coach may have completed the analysis but hasn't published the report to families yet.

Either way, it's nothing to worry about. Reports appear when they're ready, and most coaches aim to have them out within a few days of game night.
