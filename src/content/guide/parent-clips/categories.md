## The three categories

Every clip in your child's highlights belongs to one of three categories:

**Offense** — Clips where your child was involved in an offensive play. This includes carries, catches, blocks, and any other play where your child was on the field while your team had the ball.

**Defense** — Clips where your child made a notable defensive play. Tackles, interceptions, pass deflections, coverage assignments, and sacks all fall here.

**Special Teams** — Clips from kicks, punts, field goals, returns, and coverage teams. If your child plays on any special teams unit, those plays show up in this category.

## How clips are selected

The system doesn't record every single play — it identifies plays where your child was directly involved in a key action. That means a running back's highlight reel might be full of carries and receptions, while a defensive lineman's clips focus on pass rushes and stops in the backfield.

Not every snap makes the cut, and that's by design. The goal is to surface the moments that matter most, not to document every play of the game.

## Why some positions have more clips than others

It's completely normal for clip counts to vary from player to player. A quarterback or running back touches the ball frequently, so their highlight section tends to be fuller. An offensive lineman does critical work on nearly every play, but the system focuses their clips on key blocking moments — a game-changing block on a big run, or a crucial protection hold on a touchdown pass.

If your child has fewer clips than you expected, it doesn't mean they weren't playing hard. It just reflects the nature of their position and the plays that stood out on film.
