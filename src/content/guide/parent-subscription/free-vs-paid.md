## The short version

During an active season, you don't need to pay for anything. Coach Hub gives every parent on a participating team full access to their child's current season at no charge. The subscription becomes relevant when the season ends and you want to preserve access to past seasons.

## What's free

As long as your coach's Communication Hub plan is active, you have access to all of the following at no cost:

- All video clips and highlights from the current season
- All game reports published this season
- The team schedule and game results
- Team messages and announcements
- Your athlete's current season stats

You do not need a credit card or a subscription to access any of this during the active season.

## What requires a subscription ($19.99/year)

After a season ends — or if your coach's plan expires — clips and reports from past seasons become locked. A Player Profile subscription unlocks them and keeps them accessible permanently, regardless of what happens with the team or coaching staff.

With an active subscription, you also get access to the profile sharing feature, which lets you send a link to your athlete's profile to recruiters, family members, or anyone else you'd like to share it with.

## When it makes sense to subscribe

A Player Profile subscription is worth considering if:

- The current season is winding down and you want to make sure your child's highlights are preserved
- Your athlete is in high school and starting to think about playing in college — having a multi-season record in one place is valuable for recruiting
- You want to look back at past seasons for memories or to track development over time
- Your family moves between programs and you want continuity regardless of which team your athlete plays for next

## When you don't need to subscribe

If your athlete is in the middle of their first season and you're just getting started, there's no need to subscribe right now. Enjoy the free access, see how the platform works, and revisit the subscription question as the season wraps up.
