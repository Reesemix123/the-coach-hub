## Who this is for

The profile sharing feature is designed primarily for high school athletes who are starting to think about playing at the next level. A shared profile link gives college coaches, recruiters, or anyone else a clean, organized view of your athlete's highlights, stats, and game reports — all in one place.

It's also a great way to share your child's highlights with grandparents, extended family, or family friends who want to follow along.

## What a shared link shows

When you share your athlete's profile, the person viewing it will see:

- Highlights — video clips that your coach has approved and published
- Season stats — the key metrics from your athlete's profile
- Game reports — published reports from your coach

Anything that has not been approved or published by the coach will not appear. The shared link is a curated view, not a raw data dump.

## What is never shared

Coach notes that are marked as internal, unfinished reports, and any administrative data about the team are never included in a shared profile link. Your athlete's personal contact information is also not exposed through the link.

## How to get the sharing link

Look for a Share button on your athlete's profile page. This feature is coming in a future update — when it launches, you'll find the button near the top of the profile, and tapping it will give you a link you can copy and send to anyone.

## Subscription requirement

Profile sharing is available with an active Player Profile subscription ($19.99/year). This ensures that the profile has enough content — multiple seasons of clips and reports — to be worth sharing, and it helps us keep the platform running for all families.

## Privacy and control

You are always in control of what gets shared. Only content that your coach has explicitly published will appear on the shared profile. If you ever want to stop sharing, simply don't distribute the link — there is no public directory of athlete profiles.
